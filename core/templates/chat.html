The finance dataset is a comprehensive collection of historical financial data, meticulously curated to support in-depth analysis and predictive modeling. Spanning from January 2010 to December 2020, it includes daily stock prices for over 1,000 publicly traded companies listed on major stock exchanges like the NYSE and NASDAQ. Each entry captures key metrics such as daily opening, closing, high, and low prices, as well as trading volume, providing a granular view of market activity.Additionally, the dataset includes quarterly and annual financial statements, featuring over 50 financial metrics per company. These metrics encompass revenue, net income, earnings per share (EPS), dividends, return on equity (ROE), and return on assets (ROA). Balance sheet data such as total assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity are also included, along with cash flow statement items like operating cash flow, investing cash flow, and financing cash flow.To provide context for stock performance, the dataset integrates macroeconomic indicators sourced from reliable institutions. These include monthly data on interest rates, inflation rates, unemployment rates, and GDP growth rates. For a more comprehensive analysis, it also tracks sector-specific indices and benchmarks such as the S&P 500, NASDAQ Composite, and Dow Jones Industrial Average.Advanced financial metrics are included to support sophisticated analysis. These metrics include beta values, price-to-earnings (P/E) ratios, price-to-book (P/B) ratios, and market capitalization. The dataset also features data on corporate actions like stock splits, dividends, and mergers and acquisitions, which can significantly impact stock prices.
